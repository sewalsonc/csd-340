Hobbies

Blockchain Technology

Blockchain technology has become one of my biggest hobbies for the last couple of years. I got involved with buying a few different tokens in late 2018 and then shortly after I did this the market pretty much tanked. After this I sort of lost interest until the late 2019. Since then I have dedicated a lot of time looking up different chains and uses. Especially focusing on the teams and projects behind the tokens or chains they run on. I am amazed at how much this industry has grown in the last 2 years. This is a new technology that will no doubt have a fundamental impact on many parts of our everyday life.

Classic Cars

Classic cars have been a hobby of mine for over 30 years. My first classic was a 1970 Chevy C-10 truck. When I got it, it had a bad engine and transmission in it. I found a family friend that helped me change them out and I ended up driving it for the next 5 years. The next classic I got was a 1970 Chevy Chevelle and it also needed a motor. I found a running motor for it and replaced it. Even though the Chevelle was fairly rusty I was hooked on classic cars after this. Since this I have owned between 15 and 20 classics around the 1970 year. I love to restore them back to running condition first, then I redo the interiors and lastly I do the body. Usually when a car has been sitting for awhile the suspension and brakes need completely redone also. This is done in the "restore them back to running conditions step". Once it is running and driving to me it becomes a car again.

Working in the Garden

We have several acres of land near Sioux City. Around 4 or 5 acres of this is forest and the other 6 acres are open land. We live up on top of a hill so most of our land goes downhill from us. Up near the house I have made a couple differnt gardens where we like to grow tomotoes, peppers and onions. We have a bigger garden area where we do much more but I haven't used this space for a couple of years now. Another big area that we have a garden is directly down the hill from our house around 100 foot or so. In this area we have started growing fruit trees such as apple, pear and peach, along with a few fairly unknown fruit plants such as Haskap, Seaberry and Goji berry. We have had our ups and downs with our fruit garden but each year we add a little more. Someday we hope to have 3 or 4 acres of fruit trees and bushes.

Experience

Construction

Since buying our house I have had the opportunity to work on practically every aspect of it. From building the deck, putting a basement in under the house, replacing the electrical, plumbing and hvac system, to redoing most of the rooms in the place. Our house was originally built around the year 1900 and had been added on to many times before we purchased it. When we moved in it was a small two bedroom home with one bathroom. If it wasn't for the land that came with it we would of moved on to other houses to buy, that is for sure. But we took it on and 20 years later we have a 5 bedroom 2 bath house with other amenities. But we had to go through many years with major reconstruction going on.

Industrial Trainer

I have worked in the field of industrial maintenance for over 20 years and around the year 2010 I became a facility maintenance trainer. At first, the idea of being up in the front of room of people with all the focus on me wasn't what drew me to it but it was a great opportunity to advance my career and get a raise. Once I started though I was committed. Fortunately the training program was fairly new so I got to make it my own. Over the next five years or so I built my training program and made it complete with custom labs and equipment and new training curriculum. It took me the first entire year though to get totally comfortable but it finally took for me. Now I work at a college as an industrial training developer. I have now been at Western Iowa Tech Community College for around 7 years and I do much of the same that I did in my previous position but I am much busier.

Playing Music

I starting playing guitar when I was around 13 after my dad bought me a aqua blue Fender Stratocaster. It was a 51 reissue that was a few years old when I got it. I haven't spent as much time playing over the years as I would of liked but I guess life sets it's own priorities. In 2011 I met my friend Ryan through another guy named Andy. I had known Andy since I was a teenager. Andy was a drummer (Andy is the drummer in the picture above), and he was in a two piece band with Ryan. After meeting Ryan I joined up with them in the band and we were a three piece. Our band name is The Spoox and we play a little bit of about anything but country music. A few punk songs, jazz, bluesy stuff and just heavy rock. We do have a dozen or so songs of our own but after we lost Andy to a brain tumor in 2019 we kind of lost the wind from our sails. Ryan and I still play almost every weekend but we miss our crazy drummer.
